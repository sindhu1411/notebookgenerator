Cell 1: Load the fake job data sheet

raw_df = read_csv("fake_job_postings.csv")
raw_df.head()

Start by reading the sheet and checking the incoming headers, null patterns, and label column.

Cell 2: Normalize and clean columns

df.columns = normalize_headers(df.columns)
df = df.drop_duplicates(subset=["job_id"])
df["salary_range"] = df["salary_range"].fillna("not_listed")

The app mirrors this logic by normalizing headers, removing duplicate job ids, and making missing text fields explicit.

Cell 3: Build fraud-focused summaries

fraud_by_type = df.groupby("employment_type")["fraudulent"].mean()
fraud_by_logo = df.groupby("has_company_logo")["fraudulent"].mean()

Once the sheet is stable, aggregates become meaningful and highlight where suspicious postings cluster.

Cell 4: Visualize the cleaned data

plot_bar(fraud_by_type)

Key finding

Remote and low-detail postings dominate the fraudulent sample.

In this fake job sheet, the highest fraud rates appear in postings with missing company logos, vague departments, and low-information salary fields.
