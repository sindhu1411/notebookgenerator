Notebook Cleaning and Visualization Web App

Fake job posting data, cleaned and explained in a Next.js notebook-style workflow.

This web app uses a built-in fake job sheet to show how a messy tabular dataset is profiled, cleaned, and converted into visual fraud signals. The tutorial route explains each transformation step in notebook language.

NB Tutorial

01

Profile the sheet

Read the raw fake job sheet, inspect headers, and identify high-risk columns such as salary, experience, and fraud labels.

12 columns discovered in the incoming sheet.
02

Normalize and deduplicate

Standardize header names to snake_case, trim text, and keep one record per job id so the visuals are not skewed by duplicate postings.

1 duplicate row removed.
03

Repair missing values

Replace blank department, salary, employment type, and experience fields with explicit placeholders so missingness is visible and analyzable.

12 missing values resolved with deterministic fills.
04

Visualize fraud patterns

Aggregate the cleaned sheet to compare fraud rates across employment type, company logo presence, and industry mix.

35% of cleaned postings are labeled fraudulent.
